One-Time Setup Guide

Complete the four steps below to connect this document to SharePoint for persistent, shared storage. This only needs to be done once by the hiring manager. Estimated time: 10 to 15 minutes.

Step 1: Register an App in Azure AD

1

Go to portal.azure.com and sign in with your admin account (or have your IT admin do this). Navigate to Azure Active Directory > App registrations > + New registration.

2

Enter these settings: Name = Armor Onboarding Tracker. Supported account types = Accounts in this organizational directory only. Redirect URI type = Single-page application (SPA). Redirect URI value = the full SharePoint URL of this file (you can add this after uploading the file). Click Register.

3

On the app overview page, copy the Application (client) ID and Directory (tenant) ID. You will paste them into the fields below.

Step 2: Grant API Permissions

4

In the left sidebar of your app registration, click API permissions > + Add a permission > Microsoft Graph > Delegated permissions. Search for and add Files.ReadWrite and Sites.Read.All. Then click Grant admin consent.

Step 3: Prepare SharePoint

5

Upload this HTML file to a SharePoint document library (example: Shared Documents in your HR site). Note the full site URL such as https://contoso.sharepoint.com/sites/HR. The state file will be saved automatically as CRosebrook-Onboarding-State.json in that same library.

Ensure all intended users (Manager, Employee, HR, CFO, Payroll) have at least Read access to the library. The Manager and Employee accounts need Edit access.

Step 4: Enter Your Configuration

Fill in the four fields below and click Apply & Download. This generates an updated copy of the file with your values saved inside it. Upload that file to SharePoint to replace this one.

4. Management by Objectives (MBOs)

The following MBOs define expected activities, deliverables, and performance milestones during the three-month ramp period. Completion will be assessed by the Director of Sales. The employee is expected to be fully operational and performing all duties independently by June 1, 2026.

Month 1: March 2026 — Onboarding, Foundational Enablement & Initial Execution

Objectives

  • Complete all HR onboarding, security awareness, and required compliance training
  • Gain working knowledge of Armor's go-to-market motion, ICPs, buyer personas, and sales process
  • Complete systems and tools training, including HubSpot CRM and sales engagement platforms
  • Develop baseline Armor product knowledge, including MDR, SOC, SIEM, and AEC
  • Participate in cross-functional shadowing sessions with Sales and Customer Success
  • Draft initial target prospect list and outbound prospecting plan
  • Enroll in and complete The Complete Certified in Cybersecurity (CC) course (ISC2 2025)
  • Complete knowledge and awareness training for PCI DSS and HIPAA compliance frameworks
  • Begin developing persona-specific outbound messaging
  • Complete AI Training Program Module 1 (AI Fundamentals) and Module 2 (Responsible AI Use & Governance)

Month 1 Completion Criteria

  • All onboarding and training modules completed, including the ISC2 cybersecurity course
  • Systems access validated and functional across all required platforms
  • Target prospect list and outbound prospecting plan reviewed and approved by management
  • Prospecting plan reviewed with Head of Marketing
  • PCI DSS and HIPAA knowledge validation completed
  • Demonstrated understanding of compliance-driven buyer pain points and appropriate sales positioning
  • AI Foundation Modules 1 and 2 completed

Month 2: April 2026 — Messaging Development, Compliance Depth & Production Ramp

Objectives

  • Complete knowledge and awareness training for HITRUST CSF, ISO 27001, and SOC 2 (Type I and Type II)
  • Demonstrate ability to position Armor's services in support of compliance readiness and operational security
  • Create refined ICP-based prospect lists with persona-specific outbound messaging
  • Build and deploy prospect sequences in HubSpot; collaborate with Marketing to refine messaging and campaign alignment
  • Participate in role play sessions focused on discovery and objection handling; achieve mastery-level performance
  • Increase outbound prospecting activity to approximately 50-75% of full capacity
  • Generate qualified meetings aligned to ICP and compliance-driven use cases
  • Attend a minimum of two local networking or industry events
  • Complete AI Training Program: Module 3 (Prompt Engineering Basics), Module 8 (AI for Sales & Marketing), and Module 9 (Critical Oversight & Hallucination Detection)
  • Apply AI tools (Claude) to prospecting workflows, messaging development, and lead research

Month 2 Completion Criteria

  • HITRUST, ISO 27001, and SOC 2 knowledge validation completed
  • Approved messaging and sequences deployed in HubSpot
  • Outbound activity recorded at targeted capacity (50-75%)
  • Role play mastery confirmed by management
  • Qualified meetings created and documented in CRM
  • Networking event participation documented in CRM
  • AI Training Program Sales & Marketing track complete (Modules 1, 2, 3, 8, 9) with passing assessment scores (80% minimum)

Month 3: May 2026 — Full Productivity & Performance Optimization

Objectives

  • Operate at 100% outbound prospecting capacity
  • Independently manage prospecting, sequencing, and follow-up activities with minimal coaching
  • Consistently deliver high-quality, well-qualified meetings to Account Executives
  • Analyze individual performance metrics and refine targeting and messaging as needed
  • Attend a minimum of two local networking or industry events
  • Demonstrate full readiness to transition to the standard commission plan effective June 1, 2026
  • Complete AI Training Program bonus module: Mastering Claude (strongly suggested)
  • Leverage Claude and AI tools across daily prospecting, research, and communication workflows

Month 3 Completion Criteria

  • Full outbound capacity achieved and sustained throughout the month
  • Consistent meeting quality and volume meeting or exceeding established benchmarks
  • Minimal coaching intervention required for day-to-day execution
  • Management approval for transition to full commission plan participation
  • Mastering Claude bonus module completed (strongly suggested)
  • Demonstrated proficiency using AI tools in day-to-day sales workflows

5. Evaluation and Administration

  • MBO progress will be reviewed monthly by the Director of Sales, with weekly check-ins during the ramp period.
  • Failure to make reasonable progress toward MBO completion may result in modification or removal of commission offsets at the Company's discretion.
  • Armor reserves the right to amend or terminate this Addendum at any time, with or without notice, in accordance with applicable law.
